# Product

## Register

product

> App-first, but with brand sensibility. The app *is* the brand — feed, profile, settings, and search are the product surface, but the visual identity extends to any future landing/marketing page.

## Users

Spanish-speaking teens and young adults (14–25) on mobile. They open Dumpstack to scroll short videos, discover new content, interact (like, comment, save), and share their own videos. Casual entertainment context — commuting, breaks, hanging out. They value authenticity over polish.

## Product Purpose

A short-video sharing platform that feels raw and real — not corporate or algorithmically sterile. Success means users feel like they're part of a genuine community, not a content factory.

## Brand Personality

raw · auténtico · enérgico

Voice is direct, informal, real. Not polished, not corporate, not trying to be \"premium.\" The energy is raw and unfiltered — like content made by real people, not a studio.

## Anti-references

Generic AI-generated app aesthetics: no purple gradients, no glassmorphism, no identical card grids, no generic SaaS patterns. Must NOT look like a template or a startup-slop design. Should feel distinct from TikTok/Instagram (corporate-polished) and from AI-generated dashboards.

## Design Principles

1. **Content first, chrome second.** The video is the hero. UI chrome (nav, buttons, labels) recedes — dark, thin, never competes with the content.
2. **Raw over refined.** Rough edges are OK. Not every pixel needs to be perfectly balanced. Authenticity > perfection.
3. **One accent, used with intent.** Color is a scarce resource. Gray builds structure; color communicates.
4. **Dark by nature.** The app lives in darkness — dark backgrounds, dark surfaces. Not \"dark mode\" as an option, but dark as the default identity.
5. **Mobile-native.** Every decision serves a thumb-scrolling, one-handed, on-the-go experience.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Basic WCAG AA where practical: sufficient contrast (4.5:1 body, 3:1 large text), visible focus indicators, respect `prefers-reduced-motion`. No strict compliance requirements beyond good-faith effort.
